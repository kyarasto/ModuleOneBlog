The story of LoveBot
LoveBot is an art installation designed to spread warmth for Valentine's Day. Through a combination of generative elements and design choices, LoveBot gives viewers an engaging experience with love and joy :) In this blog post, I will describe my artistic vision behind LoveBot, how it is tailored to its space, the creative decisions driving its design, and the technical challenges I encountered during its creation.

The primary goal of LoveBot is to wish viewers a happy Valentine's Day with a generative element, ensuring that each viewing experience is different. I aimed for audience interaction that allows people to decipher the message conveyed, even with variations in the arrangement of words and stars. Whether the display reads "happy have valentinues a day" or "have a happy valentine's day," the meaning remains clear.

LoveBot is suspended from an air vent, creating fluid movement of the piece. To complement the Valentine's theme, I chose a pink-purple background, adorned with yellow stars for contrast. I was conflicted on whether to use stars or hearts on screen and ultimately decided I wanted the hearts to be visible from far away, so I covered the envelope in pink and purple hearts and used stars for the background on the Lovebot screen.

I wanted the whole screen to have movement, which is why I used stars and text, to create much more movement than if there were only words. The movement was also important to create a display that could capture attention with each rotation. With the envelope spinning around, each time the viewer seeâ€™s the screen on a new spin, they would be able to see clearly that it was something different. To maintain legibility and engagement, I limited the number of stars to no more than four, creating a balance between the amount of text and stars on screen.

I faced a few technical issues that other artists may run into. Ensuring that the upload speed is set to 115200 is important for the code to successfully upload. Additionally, attention to detail is really important when applying heat shrink to prevent it from slipping and shrinking on the wrong part of the wire. Another important piece of advice is to keep LoveBot in sleep mode before installation to preserve battery life. Apart from this the only other hardware difficulty was stringing the thread into the envelope. It may be tedious but be patient and you will get there!

My vision for LoveBot was to create an experience that evokes feelings of joy. Each creative decision, from the color palette to the choice of elements, was meant to represent what Valentines day feels like to me. I hope you enjoyed taking a peek into my creative process, have a happy valentines day :)
